Controls

Use the left and right arrow keys to rotate your view.

Use the up and down arrow keys to move forward and backward.

This will not work on mobile unless you know of some way to recreate the arrow keys on your phone.

Canvas? Canvas?

Explanation

Once I created two infintely extending walls, the next step was to create one infinitely extending wall that grew and shrank as you approached and withdrew from it. Rather than using the full height of the screen's edge, I just multiplied the screen's height by a function that converges to 0. I used 1/x, where x is the distance to the wall.

This approach has some drawbacks, though. Since the wall is drawn from the edge of the screen, it can't be drawn if the wall's position is inside the screen. The vanishing point automatically loops back around and makes a triange on the opposite side of the view than desired.

I needed to make a pillar at the end of the wall so I could use it instead of the edge of the screen. The issue of height was already solved. I could reuse the function for the wall height.

Since everything was based on angles, I didn't need to do any matrix or vector math to get the relative position of the pillar. All I needed to do was find the angle of the pillar relative to the view position and reuse the simple vanishing point code from earlier.

Once I had the pillar, I realized I didn't need it to draw an infinite wall. If I could join two pillars, I would have a quadrilateral that I could draw, instead of a triangle.
